Current Interests and Work

I am interested in using stars as tools to understand planet and galactic evolution. We are at an exciting time in astronomy, where the many large-scale spectroscopic and photometric surveys of stars in our Galaxy have begun to unify and entangle the sub-fields of exoplanets, stars, and galaxies. Much of my work is representative of this shift (as summarized below), including my current research with Professor Elisabeth Newton at Dartmouth College, where we are using stellar rotation to age-date stars in order to determine the duty-cycles of transient spiral arms. Stay tuned for future updates.
